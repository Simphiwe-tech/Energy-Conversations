Energy Sources

Here are mostly used renewable and non-renewable energies

Hydro Energy

Harnessing the power of flowing water, hydro energy generates electricity through turbines, offering a renewable and sustainable energy source.

Wind Energy

Utilizing the force of wind to turn turbines, wind energy provides clean and renewable electricity, playing a crucial role in reducing carbon emissions.

Solar Energy

Tapping into the abundant sunlight with photovoltaic cells, solar energy offers a clean and sustainable power source, driving advancements in renewable energy technology.

Oil Energy

Derived from fossil fuels, oil energy remains a significant contributor to global energy production, powering industries and transportation networks worldwide

Nuclear Energy

Through controlled nuclear reactions, nuclear energy produces vast amounts of electricity with minimal greenhouse gas emissions, yet it raises concerns regarding safety and nuclear waste disposal.

Thermal Energy

Leveraging heat from sources like geothermal reservoirs or industrial processes, thermal energy offers versatile applications, from heating buildings to generating electricity.

Coal Energy

Derived from fossilized plant matter, coal energy has historically been a dominant source of electricity generation, although its environmental impact, including greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution, has led to a shift towards cleaner alternatives.

Biomass Energy

Utilizing organic materials such as wood, agricultural residues, and waste to produce heat, electricity, or biofuels, biomass energy provides a renewable alternative to fossil fuels, contributing to sustainability efforts and reducing reliance on non-renewable resources.

Gas Energy

Extracted from natural gas reserves, gas energy serves as a versatile fuel for electricity generation, heating, and industrial processes, offering cleaner combustion compared to coal while also supporting the transition towards renewable energy sources.
